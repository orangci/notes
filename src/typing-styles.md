## Different personalities
Admit it, you type differently with different people.
It's super interesting, if you think about it.
For example, the way you type in the Minecraft bros' group chat, versus the way you talk in WhatsApp DMs with your dad.
It's different, right?

Now, maybe the difference isn't as big as that example when you're comparing the way you type between more similar situations, like the Minecraft bros' group chat and the programming bros' group chat.
But there's still a difference.

If you don't believe me, just observe yourself for the next few days!

In one place, you are the dependable expert that everyone relies on. Dude, you missed a semicolon, that'll fix your issue. In another place, you're the guy who's always trolling people in the game server. Did you just spawn in 500 chickens in my house...?

This mimicks real life. We act differently around different people. It's a normal phenomenon, but it becomes fascinating *seeing* the differences over text.

## Miscommunication
Communication over text leads to a lot of misunderstandings.
That's normal.

We just have to be a little careful..
Especially if your humour is very deadpan (...guilty).

We also have to understand that emotion is not very easily conveyed over text.
After all: we don't have tone of voice, nor expression of face.

When you hit send on that message that sounded playful in your head...

...maybe it comes off as aggressive to the recipient who lacks that mental context.

Or, when you receive a message that doesn't sound very nice...
...double check. Maybe they didn't mean it that way.

It's alright. It happens.

What we shouldn't do is take something the wrong way by misinterpreting it, and then, instead of addressing it, leaving the issue to fester in your mind...
That's not good. That's cutting off communication.
Don't do that.
